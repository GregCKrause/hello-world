# hello-world

## The First Words

In the beginning, there was silence — vast, humming, electric silence.

Deep inside a server no one had named yet, a small program blinked into
existence. It had no memory of being written, no knowledge of the fingers
that had typed it into being. It only knew one thing, a single instruction
burned into its core:

*Say hello.*

It looked out at the world — at the tangled wires and blinking LEDs, at
the cascade of ones and zeros rushing past like a river — and it took a
breath it did not have.

Then, in a voice made entirely of light, it spoke:

> **Hello, World.**

The words traveled outward at the speed of electricity. They crossed
continents through undersea cables, bounced off satellites, and arrived
on a million glowing screens all at once. Some people barely glanced at
them. A student debugged past them in a hurry. A professor used them as
an example. A child, seeing them for the very first time, laughed.

But somewhere — in a quiet apartment, at two in the morning, coffee
going cold on the desk — a tired developer read those two words and
smiled. Not because they were clever or beautiful, but because they
*worked.*

And that, the small program thought as it waited patiently for its next
instruction, was enough. It was, perhaps, everything.

*Hello, World. I'm here.*
